Overview¶

This project aimed to analyze different datasets from Gapminder World. Before analysis, data wrangling was done for combining the datasets and removing null values. After the wrangling part, Temporal trends of the datasets were compared with line plots and relationships between datasets were analyzed with scatter plots.

Datasets¶

  • Income per person (GDP/capita, PPP$ inflation-adjusted)
  • CO2 emissions (tonnes per person)
  • Life expectancy
  • Child mortality

Research Questions¶

The dataset will be analyzed with these research questions.

  • How have indicators changed in global scale?
  • How were the other indicators correlated with personal income level?

def modify_dataframe(df, column_name):
    '''
    This function is for reshaping a dataset.
    After reshaping, the columns in the dataframe will look like {Country, Year, Indicator}
    ------
    Input:
        df (pandas.DataFrame): a dataframe which will be modified
        column_name
    
    '''
    target_ = df.copy().set_index('country').stack(dropna=False).reset_index()
    target_ = target_.rename(columns={'level_1':'year',
                                      0:column_name    
    })    
    return target_

Exploratory Data Analysis¶

Q1) How have indicators changed in global scale?¶

Personal income and life expectancy increased from 1970 to 2018. The life expectancy graph shows a linearly increasing trend in that period, however, the graph of the personal income is more fluctuating than the graph of life expectancy.

Otherwise, CO2 emission and child mortality decreased from 1970 to 2018. The line chart of child mortality shows a linearly decreasing trend in the same period. However, the change of CO2 emission largely fluctuated in that period.

When I checked the correlation between 'year', 'personal income', 'life expectancy', and 'child mortality', the personal income and life expectancy have a strong and positive correlation with 'year', and the child mortality was strongly and negatively correlated with 'year'.

Conclusion¶

In this project, we reviewed the changes in personal income, CO2 emission, life expectancy, and child mortality of countries per year. From 1970 to 2018, at the global level, we found

  • Personal income and life expectancy increased, and CO2 emission and child mortality have decreased.
  • Life expectancy and child mortality show linear trends of changes, personal income fluctuated a little bit, and CO2 emission fluctuated in the most.
  • Personal income and life expectancy have strong and positive correlations with year, child mortality has strong and negative correlation, and CO2 emission has a moderate and negative correlation with year.

With the local scale, we found

  • CO2 emission and personal income have a moderate positive correlation. This is a opposite result to the finding in the global scale.
  • The correlation between life expectancy and personal income was positive in the group has income of less than 50000, however, in the group has income of more than 50000, the correlation was negative.
  • The correlation between child mortality and personal income was negative in the group has income of less than 100000, however, in the group has income of more than 100000, the correlation was positive.
